# 🚀 Unity Developer Career Path

> 📖 **About this document:** This is a *career-ladder* roadmap for Unity developers on the **technical track**, complementing the *topic-based* roadmap (Unity Manual & Scripting API). It is compiled from game-industry practice and the competency frameworks commonly used by studios.

The framework has **6 levels**: **Intern → Fresher → Junior → Middle → Senior → Lead**. Each level is described through 4 parts: **Role · Technical knowledge · Proof of work · Promotion criteria**.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The timelines are indicative only. Promotion is based on **demonstrated competency**, not seniority. Two people with the same 2 years of experience can sit at different levels.

---

## 🗺️ The big picture

```
INTERN ──▶ FRESHER ──▶ JUNIOR ──▶ MIDDLE ──▶ SENIOR ──▶ LEAD
 0-6mo      0-1y        1-2y       2-4y       4-7y       7y+

 "Learn      "Get the   "Ship a    "Own a     "Design    "Set the
  how to      work       full       whole      systems    direction
  learn"      done"      feature"   system"    & engine"  & lead"
```

---

## 🧭 Two growth axes that run throughout

Every promotion = expanding along two axes:

1. **Scope of impact:**
   `one line of code → one feature → one system → one project → one team`
2. **Ambiguity you can handle alone:**
   `clear task → vague problem → problem nobody has solved yet`

Keep these two axes in mind and you will always know what to sharpen to reach the next level.

---

## 📋 Promotion criteria at a glance

| Transition | Decisive question |
|---|---|
| Intern → Fresher | "Can you complete small tasks without hand-holding?" |
| Fresher → Junior | "Can you self-debug and write clean, decoupled code?" |
| Junior → Middle | "Can you ship a full feature from a vague description?" |
| Middle → Senior | "Can you own and design a whole large system?" |
| Senior → Lead | "Does your impact go beyond your own code, lifting the team?" |

---

## 📚 How to use this roadmap

* **Beginners:** read top-down from Intern; self-assess with the checklist at the end of each page.
* **Working developers:** use the "Promotion criteria" checklists as a career development plan.
* **Mentors/Leads:** use it as a competency framework and a basis for growth plans.

> [!TIP]
> Pair this career ladder with the knowledge roadmap (Unity Manual + Scripting API) and the **Refactoring / Design Patterns** tracks. The ladder answers *"how good do I need to be"*; the knowledge roadmap answers *"what to learn to get there"*.
