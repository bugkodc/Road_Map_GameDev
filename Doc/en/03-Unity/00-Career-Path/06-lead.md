# 🔴 Level 6 — Technical Lead / Lead

> ⏱️ **Indicative timeline:** 7+ years · **In one line:** *"Set technical direction & lead people."*

---

## 🎯 Role

Lead the technical effort for one team or several. The focus shifts from *"being the best coder"* to **"making the whole team better"**. You stay technically strong, but most of your time goes to **direction, people, and strategic decisions**.

---

## 🧠 Technical & leadership competencies to reach

### Technical vision
* Set a **long-term technical roadmap** for the team/studio.
* Choose architecture & tech stack; weigh **build vs buy**.

### Org-level technical debt
* Balance **delivery speed ↔ foundation quality**.
* Decide when to "borrow" technical debt and plan repayment.

### System design review
* Review Seniors' designs; act as the **gatekeeper** for architectural quality.
* Define the "Definition of Done" and quality standards.

### Growing people
* **Mentoring**; build growth plans for each member.
* Assess competencies; **hiring** & technical interviewing.

### Engineering process
* Establish **CI/CD**, a **code-review** culture, manage the project's technical risk.

### Multi-directional communication
* Represent engineering with **Producers / Publishers / Stakeholders**.
* **Estimate & plan** technical work; report risk in business language.

### Stay sharp
* Stay deep enough to **jump in on hard problems** and judge the team's decisions correctly.

---

## 🌿 Career branches from Lead

```
                 ┌─▶ Principal / Staff Engineer ─▶ Software Architect ─▶ Technical Director (studio CTO)
   LEAD ─────────┤   (Pure technical — IC track)
                 │
                 └─▶ Engineering Manager ─▶ Studio / Department Head
                     (People-management track)
```

* **Pure technical (IC):** keep going deep, tackle the biggest architecture problems, manage fewer people.
* **Management track:** focus on people, budget, organization, and product strategy.

---

## 🛠️ Proof of work

* Led the technical effort of ≥1 project/team to a successful ship.
* Have mentees who were **promoted**.
* Established processes/standards the organization sustains over time.

---

> [!IMPORTANT]
> At Lead level, the measure of success is **no longer the code you write**, but **the results of the people you lead** and the durability of the systems/processes you leave behind.
