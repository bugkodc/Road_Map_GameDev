## 🏛️ Origins in Building Architecture

An interesting fact is that the concept of a **Design Pattern** was not invented by computer programmers, but originated with an Austrian-American building architect named **Christopher Alexander**.

In 1977, Alexander and his colleagues published the famous book *"A Pattern Language: Towns, Buildings, Construction"*.
- In this book, he compiled and described recurring design solutions for solving problems in urban planning and house construction (for example, how to arrange window heights, where to place a garden, the structure of neighborhood walkways).
- His core idea was: **The most beautiful communities and buildings have always been built upon design templates that have proven effective over the centuries.**

---

## 💻 Adoption into Computer Science

In the late 1980s, software engineers began to realize that Christopher Alexander's ideas could be fully applied to designing object-oriented (OOP) code structures.

In 1994, a historic turning point occurred when the legendary book **"Design Patterns: Elements of Reusable Object-Oriented Software"** was published by four authors:
1.  **Erich Gamma**
2.  **Richard Helm**
3.  **Ralph Johnson**
4.  **John Vlissides**

These four authors quickly became known worldwide by the nickname **"Gang of Four" (GoF)**.

> [!NOTE]
> The GoF book introduced exactly **23 foundational design patterns** (nowadays often summarized as 22 due to consolidation), scientifically divided into **3 main groups**: Creational, Structural, and Behavioral. It is considered the "bible" that laid the foundation for modern object-oriented software architecture.

---

## 🎮 Impact on Game Dev

The Gang of Four's book shaped the way we write professional game code today.
- Many years ago, game code was often written in a patched-together procedural style, leading to game projects that were extremely difficult to extend or upgrade graphically.
- The popularity of Design Patterns drove the emergence of component-oriented game engines like **Unity (Component-based)** and **Unreal Engine (Gameplay Framework)**. The architects who developed these engines embedded the GoF design patterns directly into the core of the engine so that millions of game developers around the world could use them easily.
