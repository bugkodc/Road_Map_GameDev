# 📝 Engineering Blogs

Reading engineering blogs from tech industry leaders is one of the best ways to learn how real scalability, resilience, and operational challenges are solved at production scale.

---

### 1. Netflix TechBlog
*   **Specialization:** Pioneers of Chaos Engineering (Chaos Monkey), High Availability (HA) architectures, microservices at massive scale, and low-latency video content distribution networks.
*   **Link:** [Netflix TechBlog (Medium)](https://netflixtechblog.com)

---

### 2. Uber Engineering Blog
*   **Specialization:** Real-time stream processing infrastructures, large-scale geospatial platforms, and optimized microservices frameworks.
*   **Link:** [Uber Engineering](https://www.uber.com/blog/engineering/)

---

### 3. Discord Engineering Blog
*   **Specialization:** Highly relevant for Real-Time & Game Systems. Details how to scale WebSockets for millions of concurrent connections, migrations from MongoDB to Cassandra/ScyllaDB, and building ultra-high performance services in Rust.
*   **Link:** [Discord Engineering](https://discord.com/blog/categories/engineering)

---

### 4. Cloudflare Blog
*   **Specialization:** Computer networking protocols, infrastructure security (DDoS mitigation), global DNS routing, Edge Computing (Cloudflare Workers), and kernel-level hardware optimizations.
*   **Link:** [Cloudflare Blog](https://blog.cloudflare.com)

---

### 5. Meta Engineering (Engineering at Meta)
*   **Specialization:** Ultra-high performance distributed caching (Memcached at scale), TAO (distributed social graph database), software development tooling, and large-scale AI cluster infrastructures.
*   **Link:** [Meta Engineering](https://engineering.fb.com)

---

### 6. Slack Engineering
*   **Specialization:** Real-time message push systems, message queuing pipeline optimizations, and operational post-mortems of distributed messaging networks.
*   **Link:** [Slack Engineering](https://slack.engineering)

---

### 7. AWS Architecture Blog
*   **Specialization:** Cloud computing design patterns, serverless architectures, multi-region active-active deployments, and cost-effective disaster recovery.
*   **Link:** [AWS Architecture Blog](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/architecture/)
